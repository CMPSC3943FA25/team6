Simple lifestyle changes for a healthier heart

Eat More Fiber

Include whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and legumes in your diet. Fiber helps lower cholesterol and maintain healthy blood pressure.

Reduce Sodium

Limit salt intake to less than 2,300mg per day. Use herbs and spices for flavoring instead of salt.

Healthy Fats

Choose unsaturated fats from olive oil, nuts, and fish. Limit saturated and trans fats found in fried and processed foods.

Portion Control

Maintain a healthy weight by controlling portion sizes and eating mindfully.

Aerobic Exercise

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week. Walking, swimming, or cycling are great options.

Strength Training

Include muscle-strengthening activities at least 2 days per week. This helps maintain a healthy metabolism.

Stay Active Daily

Take the stairs, park farther away, or have walking meetings. Every bit of movement counts!

Stretch & Relax

Practice yoga or stretching to reduce stress and improve flexibility.

üßò Lifestyle Changes

Quit Smoking

Smoking significantly increases heart disease risk. Seek support from cessation programs if needed.

Manage Stress

Practice meditation, deep breathing, or hobbies you enjoy. Chronic stress affects heart health.

Get Good Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night. Poor sleep is linked to heart problems.

Limit Alcohol

If you drink, do so in moderation. Excessive alcohol can raise blood pressure and cholesterol.

üìä Regular Monitoring

Check Blood Pressure

Monitor your blood pressure regularly, especially if you have a family history of hypertension.

Know Your Numbers

Get regular checkups and know your cholesterol, blood sugar, and BMI numbers.

Follow Up

Keep appointments with your healthcare provider and follow their recommendations.

Track Progress

Keep a journal of your health metrics, diet, and exercise to see improvements over time.

üö® Warning Signs - Seek Immediate Medical Attention

  • Chest pain or discomfort that doesn't go away
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain or discomfort in jaw, neck, back, arms, or stomach
  • Lightheadedness, nausea, or cold sweats
  • Irregular heartbeat that persists
  • Sudden severe headache

If you experience any of these symptoms, call emergency services immediately!
